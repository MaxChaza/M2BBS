NC = netclass

This property shows the netclass of the net. The netclass can be one of the following:

  • SM: state-machine;
  • MG: marked graph;
  • SM & MG: state-machine and marked graph;
  • FC: free choice;
  • EFC: extended free choice;
  • ES: extended simple;
  • NC: no specific netclass.

State-Machine

A Petri net is a state-machine if all transitions have exactly one pre- and exactly one post-place.

Mathematically speaking: ∀ t ∊ T : |•t| = |t•| = 1.

Marked Graph

A Petri net is a marked graph if all places have exactly one pre- and exactly one post-transition.

Mathematically speaking: ∀ p ∊ P : |•p| = |p•| = 1.

Free Choice

A Petri net is a free choice net if all places have either at most one post-transition or it is the only pre-place of a transition.

Mathematically speaking: ∀ p ∊ P : |p•| = 1 ⋁ •(p•) = {p}.

Extended Free Choice

A Petri net is an extended free choice net if all transitions in conflict have identical sets of pre-places.

Mathematically speaking: ∀ p,q ∊ P : p• ∩ q• = ∅ ⋁ p• = q•.

Extended Simple

A Petri net is extended simple if every transitions is involved in conflict at most.

Mathematically speaking: ∀ p,q ∊ P : p• ∩ q• = ∅ ⋁ p• ⊆ q• ⋁ q• ⊆ p•.
